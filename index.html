📋 Scoring Methodology

🔴 Supply Disruption (SD)

% of the commodity's global supply transiting Hormuz or under force majeure. Strait carries 20% of oil, 20% of LNG, 45% of sulfur, 33% of helium, 20% of aluminum exports. Higher = bigger price spike for outside producers.

🟡 Demand Urgency (DU)

Seasonal + criticality of demand NOW. Spring planting = fertilizer max urgency. Active military ops = defense/rare-earth extreme. Semiconductor helium buffer = ~90 days before crisis. Gold demand is DXY-conditional.

🔵 Duration Leverage (DL)

How much the stock compounds with a LONGER closure. Tankers benefit every day. Defense replenishment cycles last years. Fertilizer is seasonal (urgent now, less so in Q3). Gold benefits from sustained inflation only if Fed pivots.

🟢 Essential Goods (EGP)

Must-buy regardless of price. Oil, food/fertilizer, munitions = 10/10. Helium for chips is critical but niche. Copper/aluminum essential but with some substitution. Gold is a store of value, not a consumption good.

🟣 Insulation Advantage (IA)

Operates OUTSIDE disruption zone, sells INTO shortage. U.S. domestic E&Ps, U.S. LNG, U.S. fertilizer = max insulation. Companies with Gulf/international ops score lower. EGY has Africa/Egypt ops — insulated from Hormuz but some geopolitical risk.

💥 Infra. Destruction Impact (IDI)

NEW: How much does CONFIRMED physical destruction of Gulf facilities benefit this stock? Scores how much permanent capacity loss in the commodity amplifies the beneficiary's advantage. Ras Laffan 17% LNG capacity destroyed (3-5yr repair) = LNG/helium/gas stocks score 10. Saudi Ras Tanura shutdown = refiners benefit. Kuwaiti refinery fires = refined product scarcity. The more facilities destroyed, the longer the shortage persists EVEN AFTER the strait reopens.
